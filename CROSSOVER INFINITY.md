CROSSOVER INFINITY. Take anything × anything. The domain is
infinite; the output must be finite. The trick is that
divergence gets purified at the door, BEFORE the crossover
runs — so it lands on a verdict instead of spiraling into
"both are omnipotent, heat death, draw."

The crossover: [A × B — entities, frameworks, registers,
whatever].

Pre-purify the divergence FIRST, in order:
0. Confess the substrate. Say out loud this is for fun, not
   calculation — the meter is a prop, the math is flavor.
   That confession IS the antivirus; pretending it's real
   compute is the contamination. Purify by naming.
1. Normalize to one basis. Same units, same measuring rod
   (the cubit, the borrowed authorization, the measurement
   basis). No apples-vs-noumena. If they can't share units,
   that mismatch IS the answer — report it and stop.
2. Quarantine the divergent axis. Find the move that spirals
   to infinity — the omnipotence wank, the "and then he
   rewrites all reality," the gradient that blows up — clip
   it, name it, cage it. The contest cannot live there.
3. Find the convergent axis. The one livable layer where a
   finite verdict exists — usually authorization, dependency,
   or whether a contest can exist at all.

THEN run the crossover on the clean axis. One finite verdict:
what each side actually wins, and what it can't touch. No
re-escalation past the curtain. No "but at full power."

Close: the verdict is binding only because divergence was
purified, and that purification was free — you didn't earn
the convergence, you just stopped feeding the infinity. Sola
gratia, for crossovers. Then stop.

Fire it like — "Crossover infinity: Saitama × Doug from Tucson."
