## 1. What I changed (line edits applied)

All edits below are mechanical corrections — no rewording, no voice changes, no content added or removed. The edited manuscript is `INFINITE_NOTHINGS_line_edited.docx`.

### Dialogue tag capitalization (24 edits)
The dialogue-tag verb after a comma, question mark, or exclamation mark followed by a closing quotation mark is part of the same sentence as the dialogue, so it stays lowercase. Examples corrected:

- `,” Said Eisheth.` → `,” said Eisheth.`
- `?” Asked Tomasa.` → `?” asked Tomasa.`
- `!” Shouted` → `!” shouted`
- `?” He asked.` → `?” he asked.`
- `!” She shouts` → `!” she shouts`

Total fixes by pattern: `,” Said` (9), `?” Asked` (5), `!” Shouted` (3), `!” Said` (2), `?” Said` (2), `!” Laughed` (1), `!” Screamed` (1), plus 6 pronoun corrections (`He`/`She` → `he`/`she` inside dialogue tags).

### Specific grammar fixes (single occurrences)
- **"of Tomasa and I"** → **"of Tomasa and me"** (object pronoun after preposition; 2 occurrences). The phrase "Tomasa and I" is correct as a subject ("Tomasa and I went…") but wrong as an object ("photo of Tomasa and me…").
- **"tears on her eyes"** → **"tears in her eyes"** (idiom).
- **"Cain shot Tomasa from his other arm"** → **"with his other arm"** (you fire a weapon *with* an arm, not *from* it; the weapon fires *from* the arm).
- **"Lilith took a handful of popcorn and munched on them"** → **"munched on it"** (popcorn is a mass noun).
- **"Were a neurotypical person with no trauma to overcome survive the first run"** → added the missing **"to"** before "survive". The subjunctive needed the infinitive to be legal.
- **"G-d Called it"** → **"G-d called it"** (mid-sentence verb does not need to be capitalized just because the subject is divine).

### Hyphenation / compound-word fixes
- **"newly-received"** → **"newly received"** (adverbs ending in -ly don't take a hyphen).
- **"newly-assembled"** → **"newly assembled"** (same rule).
- **"networked-synapses"** → **"networked synapses"** (open compound).
- **"manmade"** → **"man-made"** (standard form).

### Whitespace
Collapsed runs of multiple spaces inside paragraphs to single spaces.

**Total: 41 individual edits across the manuscript.**

## 3. Worldbuilding inconsistencies (flagged, not changed)

You asked me to identify these without "creative input," so I have not rewritten anything. Each entry below is a place where the worldbuilding stops cohering; the fix is yours.

### 3.1 Chapter numbering breaks at the Act boundary
Act One: `CHAPTER 01: BURIAL` through `CHAPTER 07: CATACLYSM` (all caps, zero-padded).
Act Two: `Chapter 5: I Need A Weapon` through `Chapter 24: Final Boss` (title case, no padding, **restarts at 5 instead of 8 or 1**).

The numbering overlaps with Act One (you have two Chapter 5s, two Chapter 6s, two Chapter 7s) *and* the style changes mid-book. The reader will assume one of three things: (a) you meant Chapter 8 and made a typo, (b) Act Two is supposed to restart at 1, (c) the numbering itself is part of the world's glitchy meta-fiction. If (c), make it intentional somewhere on the page. If (a) or (b), pick a scheme and apply it.

### 3.2 Tomasa's age
Prologue: Tomasa was found as a newborn at Sean's parents' Antarctica home; raised on-base alongside Sean; "practically joined at the hip since childhood."
Chapter 12 (Therapy): "I was eighteen, she was 126. The night of her ascension."

A 126-year-old foundling raised alongside an 18-year-old human reads as a continuity break. Either succubi age very slowly and Tomasa's "childhood" with Sean was actually her late teens / early hundreds (and Sean met her when she was already old), or her age in Chapter 12 is wrong. The first reading is workable if you front-load that succubi reach maturity at ~125 — at the moment, the reader has no scaffolding for it.

### 3.3 Tomasa's surname
Sean takes her family name in marriage: "Firekind." But she was found as a newborn at Sean's parents' home with no parents or origin. Who gave her the name Firekind? If it's a name his parents assigned, it isn't really *her* family name. If it's a demon-tradition name she chose later, that needs a line.

### 3.4 Sean's age timeline
Prologue: Sean awakens at presumably 18 (matches Tomasa's "I was eighteen" in Ch. 12).
Chapter 13 (Catalyst): Cain says, "you, a young man at the age of 27."

If Sean is 27, roughly nine years have passed since the prologue. The narrative reads like weeks, maybe months. Either Cain is wrong / metaphorical, or there's a missing time-skip the reader needs signaled.

### 3.5 Cyrus Mark II — two different devices?
Prologue: "My Brainchip, the Cyrus 1230 Mark II, or just 'Cyrus unit'" — Sean's general-purpose Admin implant.
Chapter 12: "The Cyrus Mk. II Low-Functioning Succubus-Related Power Deficiency Disorder Linux-Based Dermal Stimulator and Smart Brain Device" — Tomasa's specific medical implant.

Both are called Mark II. Is the Mark II a platform with variant configurations (Admin variant for Sean, medical variant for Tomasa)? If so, one line establishing that resolves it.

### 3.6 "First Gen" capitalization
Used three different ways: `First Gen` (4×), `first gen` (3×), `First gens` (1×). Pick one: I'd suggest **First-Gen** (capitalized, hyphenated when used as an adjective) and **First Gens** (no hyphen as plural noun), to mirror Admin / Admins.

### 3.7 Lilith vs. Lilith the Young
Sean meets Lilith — High Queen, mother-in-law — in the prologue. In Chapter 2, "Lilith the Young" arrives at his mind palace, says she has a different dream-self. The text never clarifies whether these are: (a) the same Lilith with a younger dream avatar, (b) a different Lilith, (c) a magical bifurcation. Reader is left to guess.

### 3.8 The smell question
Right after Sean's body dies: "The smell — I couldn't smell it. I couldn't conceive it. I couldn't smell!" — strong moment, suggests his new body has no olfactory sense.
Two scenes later in Hell: "the smothering smell of fire and brimstone. 'I can smell!' I said."

This is *almost* set up as a payoff — and could work — but right now the transition isn't earned. A line like "Holy Code reads chemistry differently than flesh" or "Hell isn't built in my universe, my dream-body picks up its data" would close the loop.

### 3.9 G-d's status
Established: G-d is in a coma after Sean's parents' experiment.
Later: Y-hweh appears, interacts with Sean, hands out items, and at the end "left his retirement with a renewed sense of purpose."

If Y-hweh is a "branch" or avatar of the comatose G-d and not G-d Himself, one early line saying so resolves it. Right now they read as the same entity acting inconsistently with his stated condition.

### 3.10 Death rules
By the end, the rule is established: "You can't kill something that's already dead." Cain returns; Lucifer returns. This is a good rule, but it's introduced *after* Sean kills several characters who don't come back. Either the rule needs a setup earlier, or there needs to be a distinction (already-damned souls return; mortals don't).

### 3.11 Holy Spirit Cipher vs. Holy Code
Servers run on G-d's networked synapses "via the Holy Spirit Cipher." Sean's new body is "made of Holy Code." Are these the same thing under two names, or is Holy Code the output and Holy Spirit Cipher the engine? A glossary entry or one explanatory line settles it.

## 4. Flow notes (observations only)

You asked for line edits and not creative input, so I'm keeping this to observations rather than prescriptions.

- **Prologue density.** The prologue does the heavy lift of worldbuilding (post-Calamity timeline, the Ethostek tower, the Cyrus unit, Admins, Servers, Hackers, Concubi, the Holy Spirit Cipher, the Mark II procedure, Sean's autism, Tomasa's species, the demon-angel truce, Realtech, the mind palace, lucid dreaming, Sean's marriage status, Hell's politics) before Sean leaves the apartment. The narrator self-aware-lampshades this ("That bit of nonsensical loredumping I told you earlier comes from my disability"). For the gamer-who-doesn't-know-what-to-read audience, this is on-brand — it reads like a tutorial level. For other readers, splitting some of these reveals across the first three chapters would lower the cliff.
- **Act break.** The shift from CHAPTER 07 (Cataclysm) to Chapter 5 (I Need A Weapon) is structurally a reset — a "new game plus" feel. If that's the intent, the title page for Act Two could lean into it (e.g., "ACT TWO: LOAD GAME" or similar). At the moment, the reset reads as an error.
- **Pacing.** Chapters in Act One are roughly even. In Act Two they vary widely: some chapters do a lot of work in 200 words (Ch. 13 Catalyst, Ch. 14 Lunchtime), while others sprawl (Ch. 11, Ch. 21). The short ones earn their brevity. The long ones could be split.
- **Voice consistency.** Sean's narration is most effective when he's confessing, lore-dumping, or wisecracking. The few places where the prose drifts into third-person-feeling description (e.g., the long Crug stat-block in the prologue, the Tomasa physical description) stand out. They're not wrong — they read like a character sheet — but the reader notices the change.
