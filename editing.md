# editing.md — *INFINITE NOTHINGS: ADMIN, Book One*

Working revision plan. Ordered by leverage: do Priority 1–2 before anything cosmetic.

---

## North Star (the one rule)

**Sean's power = his self-perception.** The system, the theme, and the arc are one object. Every edit either tightens that weld or it doesn't ship. When a scene, a power, or a save makes Sean stronger *without* making him more whole, it's working against the book even if it's cool.

---

## Priority 1 — The back half spends the constraint that powers the front half

**Problem.** The most dramatically alive Sean is the powerless one (Cain, Ch. 12 — the lock is psychological, so the fight is internal and it *lands*). As he climbs to "Developer / absolute manipulative control / COBALT −1 = infinite," the governor comes off and tension leaks. The text already half-knows ("anticlimactic"; "I invented climaxes").

**Decide, don't drift.** Two coherent routes:

- **(A) Lean into deflation as theme.** Keep the anticlimaxes but make them *say something* — God's fights are anticlimactic on purpose because raw power was never the point. Then the human-scale beats (testimony, choice, Tomasa) must carry every climax, and they have to be staged as the real victories.
- **(B) Keep the governor meaningful to the end.** Make "infinite" cost something visible each time it's used (the Ch. 27 line about the suit barely surviving its own power is the seed — grow it). Power that hurts to hold stays dramatic.

**Either way:** the one lock only *Sean* can pick belongs at the climax's center. Right now the final boss is beaten by a revolver of "concentrated superstrings"; the *human* turn (he stops agreeing with the void) should be the thing that actually fires the gun.

---

## Priority 2 — Deploy `testify_to_angels.md` as a structural device

The therapy-log voice is the interiority the back half is missing. Use fragments as **act interstitials** so the COBALT −1 climax reads as *self-acceptance*, not a bigger number.

**Placement map (proposed):**

| Slot | Fragment | Job |
|---|---|---|
| After Ch. 01 (Burial) | the urn / survivor's-ledger passage | plant the wound early |
| End of Act One | the "shard of glass / service as armor" beat | name the lock before the plot obscures it |

Keep them short and clearly marked (the Cyrus-log framing already gives you a clean device). Don't let them become more loredumping — they're *feeling* data, not lore.

---

## Priority 3 — On-ramp / front-loading

The Prologue dumps the entire cosmology; the Act One mana/orgasm-typology runs long. The narrator lampshades it ("nonsensical loredumping… comes from my disability"), which is charming and on-theme but is still the steepest part of the climb.

- Cut the Prologue's exposition to what the *scene* needs (Sean waking, the procedure, Tomasa, the funeral). Let the rest arrive when it's load-bearing.
- The strongest early scenes are relational (Lilith drying off; the therapy bench; the Agrat "date"). Trust those to teach the world. Convert at least one big explainer (the three-queens mana lecture) into something *dramatized* rather than slide-deck'd.

---

## Priority 4 — Deus ex machina audit

It's named and embraced — and because God is a character and improvisation is His established trait, you have more license than almost anyone. But each visible author's-hand save that *rescues* Sean (vs. one he *triggers*) trades reader investment for a laugh.

- Tag every save in the final act as **EARNED** (Sean caused it) or **ARRIVED** (it showed up).
- Convert enough ARRIVED → EARNED that the protagonist's agency peaks at the climax. Allies carrying load is fine and on-theme ("you can't do it alone") — but the *decisive* move stays Sean's.

---

## Priority 5 — Register control on the heaviest material

Hitler-as-detective-then-executed, the Holocaust as recurring theodicy fulcrum, the Deep's sexual-violence content. Sincerity earns most of the darkness; a few beats wobble between satire and gravity.

- Pick the register per beat *on purpose*. The wobble can be the point (Cruelty-Squad-adjacent), but it should be a choice, not a tonal accident.
- Specifically pressure-test the Hitler-detective sequence: is the bit worth what it costs the surrounding gravity? If yes, commit harder to the satire; if no, give the forensic function to a non-comic figure.

---

## Priority 6 — Continuity / line-level

- **Ninil.** Set up as important (draws the seal that saves Sean; taken in the Cataclysm) and never resolved. Pay it off (a Book Two thread?) or trim the setup so it doesn't promise.
- **COBALT has two expansions** in-text: "Code-Operation Basic Admin Level of Trust" (Ch. 06) vs. "Cyrus Overpoweredness-Based Automatic Limiting Technology" (Ch. 09). Pick one — or make the discrepancy an explicit Cyrus/unreliable-narrator gag so it reads as intentional.
- **Tomasa-2 / "Two"** is a strong twist but arrives late to be load-bearing for the respawn mechanics. Seed the "emergency soul backup" idea earlier (a line in Act One) so the reveal feels planted, not patched.

---

## Book Two seeds (optional, for later)

You left a loaded board: Two needs a real body and a real arc; the Dreamlands are mid-assimilation; Heaven 3 is half-built; AI-0 and Vishnu can return "with the right resources"; Ninil is unaccounted for; Tiqva ("hope") exists. The cleanest throughline for Book Two is the inverse of Book One's: Book One was *can Sean believe he's worth saving* — Book Two could be *can Sean build/govern without becoming the thing he beat* (the Developer-as-Demiurge temptation). The metaphysics already supports it.
