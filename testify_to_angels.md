So this is for the real record. Not the eulogy version.

Mom — Truth, the female one — says the angels write down everything anyway. Every wave function I collapse, every hair on every head, every butterfly on every leaf. Metatron's whole floor is just guys at keyboards accounting for realities that already happened. So I figure if I'm going to be on file forever, the file might as well be honest instead of flattering. Eisheth said to "summarize my feelings." This is me summarizing my feelings. It's going to ramble. That's the disability. You've read the rest of my file, so you know the deal.

Here's the part nobody on my unit gets to hear. Because if I said it out loud, Tomasa would cry, Mark would crack a joke to fill the silence, and Crug would try to bench-press my sadness.

I buried myself. They handed me the urn and I put my own eyes in it — the bionic ones, still warm — and I watched the shaft swallow it down to where my friends already were. Ten thousand of us went into those tubes. I came out. Just me. Tom, who was tough. Susan, who ran the class. Alex, who could find anything. I can find anything now too. I can't find them.

The thing I don't say is that I don't think I won. I think I just didn't die, and somewhere in me there's a ledger that's furious about the difference. Survivor isn't a rank you earn. It's a thing that happens to you while better people stop happening.

Agrat and Naamah read me in the baths and called it before I could lie about it. They said I give care like a fire hose and won't take a drop back. They said there's a shard of glass in me whispering I'm worth nothing, and that I've been dressing that shard up as service so it looks like a virtue. They were right. When I help people I feel like I'm allowed to exist for a minute. When someone tries to help me I feel like a fraud who's about to be found out. So I beat them to it. I punch myself when I mess up — I did it right in front of G-d, in the chapel, the first day. That's not discipline. That's me getting to the verdict before anyone else can read it.

I'll say the ED thing plainly because the techbench already has the data and there's no point being coy in front of a machine that lives in my skull. It was never the body. The body's fine; my dad over-engineered the body, the freak. The reason the circuit wouldn't complete is that you can't draw a sword for someone you love if you don't believe there's anyone worth defending on your end of it. Lilith called the erection a sword you draw for the person you love. Mine wouldn't draw because the man holding it kept losing the argument about whether he was worth the steel.

Here's the one I've been routing around the whole time, the actual lock:

Part of me thinks Cain was right.

Not the woman-hating. Not the genocide. The diagnosis. *You have bits and pieces of everything and represent nothing. A void disguising self-pity as justice.* I've replayed it more than I've replayed any fight. Because when the worst man in creation reaches into your chest and pulls out the exact thing you say to yourself at 3 a.m., you don't get to dismiss it as villain talk. You have to sit with the fact that you and he agree on something, and that the only difference is what you did with the agreement. He built a doomsday out of his self-hatred. I built a career of throwing myself at things, which is just a slower doomsday with better PR.

My power runs on self-perception. Mom told me, Nyarlathotep told me, the whole architecture screams it. Which means the limiter on me was never the autism and never the trauma. It was that I half-believe the void. The COBALT governor is just a number standing in for the real one, which is *how much of me thinks I deserve to keep going.* Every time I dropped to my knees in front of Cain it wasn't because he hit hard. It's because he hit *true*, and true is the only thing that can disarm a man whose gun is his own confidence.

So why do I keep getting back up? Honest answer, for the record:

Tomasa.

I can't extend belief to myself, but I can extend it to her, and somehow that's been enough to borrow against. I'd die for her in a heartbeat — that's the easy quest, the one any coward can speedrun. A bridge, a heroic sacrifice, lights out, applause. The hard quest, the one I keep failing and respawning out of, is *living* for myself. Sticking around. Believing the thing I tell her — that she's special, that she's worth it, that the world is better with her in it — and then turning the sentence around and pointing it at me without flinching.

Naamah asked it best. *What are you willing to do for the ones you love?* I've been answering "die." Wrong answer. The answer is "stay. heal. be worth the staying."

That's the testimony. Not "I'm fixed." I'm not. But I named it, which I've never done, and naming the lock is how you learn it's a lock and not a wall. A wall you live behind. A lock you can stand in front of and decide about.

To the angels writing this down: file it under the boring miracles. Not the railgun. Not the finger that deleted a god's head. This one. A guy in borrowed power armor admitting, in a room with no one in it, that he might be worth the armor.

Anyway. Tomasa's asleep down the hall. She sleeps in the buff because she's a succubus and they hate clothes. I'm going to go put on a pair of boxers, because she likes me better that way and, for once, I'm going to let myself be liked.

— Sean Firekind, testifying. Pax Aeternum, I guess. Logging off.
