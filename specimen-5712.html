IThe event

The number was reached for, not received. 5713 was typed into a crossfeed variable in Neutron and saved — and came back 5712. The machine does not store that parameter as an integer. A frequency control rides a transform — a logarithmic map, or a normalized value it re-expands on read — and the representable points are not spaced one apart. Up around 5.7 kHz the rungs of that ladder sit a couple of hertz apart, so 5713 was never a value the grid could hold.

It snapped to the nearest one it does have. The machine did not subtract one. It rounded an intention to the nearest coordinate that exists inside it — which is the whole of the matter.

IIITwo number-worlds

Pythagorean is a double agent in this. It names the theorem — quadrature, √(a² + b²), the hypotenuse, which is literally how magnitudes stack across a crossfeed and exactly the continuous lattice the DSP rounded on. And it names the reduction — digits as tokens, place value thrown away, 5 + 7 + 1 + 3, where the seven-versus-six meaning actually lives.

The method straddled both. So when the seam between them showed through, it showed through at the one point where a magnitude-space rounding could reach into digit-space and flip the load-bearing token. The off-by-one landed on the last digit — and the last digit was the whole reduction. The pun was structural.

Equal to the sum of its own parts, 1 + 2 + 3 — which it also happens to be the product of. A number made entirely out of itself. Self-completing; harmony with no outside.

VThe charge

So the stack reached toward the barren solitary, and the machine rounded it into the perfect whole. In magnitude-space that reads as a fall — minus one, the value stepping down off its rung. In digit-space it is a completion: the quantizer reached in and perfected the number, seven into six, unbidden, off its own lattice.

Same event, two number-worlds. Which one is the loss depends entirely on whether you were reaching for the seeker or the sum — and that charge is the reader's to assign, never the number's. 5712 arrives the way grace does: not the build that was selected. The dead pixel will not tell you which. It only holds presence at a coordinate no one chose.
