## The Tiering System

INFINITE NOTHINGS doesn't scale by output (who hits hardest). It scales by **proximity to the substrate** — how close a thing sits to the Holy Code reality is rendered from, expressed as CPU **protection rings**. Output is the *floor*, not the ceiling.

| Tier | Ring | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **Versal** | Ring 3 (userland) | Raw force. Fights inside the rules. |
| **Admin** | sudo | Edits reality with scoped permission. |
| **Root / Developer** | Ring 0 (a server) | Total write-access. Solves by force, pays the cost. |
| **Kernel** | Ring 0 (of all reality) | The gate-and-key. Can't be climbed — only befriended. |
| **Off-axis: Entropy** | — | Corrupts the substrate. The reason stakes are real. |
| **Off-axis: Control** | — | Moves players without touching the board. |
| **Transcendence** | above the server | *Is* or *owns* the frame. The dreaming substrate. |

**The unseen ceiling:** above every reachable tier sit G-d (the dreamer) and the 12th-dimensional exiles. No on-page character reaches it.

### Interaction Rules (how matchups actually resolve)
- **R1** — Editing the rules beats fighting inside them.
- **R2** — Living off-map ≠ winning by off-map rules.
- **R3** — Corruption is *stalled* by containment, *ended* only by legitimate authority that revokes its access.
- **R4** — Authority is bounded by jurisdiction. Root on your server ≠ root on the network.
- **R5** — Deletion needs something to delete. The "already dead" can't be killed.
- **R6** — Manipulation needs a will. The mindless can't be bargained with — or threatened.
- **R7** — The kernel is befriended, not beaten. *(Keystone rule.)*

## PROTAGONISTS

### Sean Firekind
*The Narrator · Second-Generation Admin · later the Developer · later Creation*

- **Tier:** Varies enormously by Key — **Versal** at base, scaling to **Transcendence (breaching)** at endgame.
- **Classification:** Human reborn as Holy Code; autistic; husband of Tomasa; father of Tiqva.
- **Key:** *Pre-Transformation* | *Cyrus / Admin* | *NIGHTMARE* | *Developer (Root)* | *Book-Two Pistol-Start* | *Creation*

**Powers & Abilities (scaling by Key):**
- *Cyrus / Admin (sudo):* direct code-reading, ticrate manipulation (froze an enemy's emulated tickrate and bisected it), imagination-to-matter via the Mark II dream processor, self-authored powers (accidentally wrote himself precognition), respawn anchored to Tomasa's Mind Palace. COBALT-limited (level 7→6).
- *NIGHTMARE (overclock):* releases the limiter for "nearly G-dlike power, aleph-aleph repeating." **Drawback:** damage taken equals damage spent; no permanent growth. A spike, not a state.
- *Developer (Root):* ate the silver key — "the encryption method to all space and time." Writes code at will; immortal ("I shall never die"); ran COBALT *past zero* to Aleph-Negative-One.
- *Book-Two Pistol-Start:* **deliberately de-scaled.** Hands back root, restarts at userland with one sidearm — the admin gun, now with a trigger (can choose *not* to fire). Two kinds of aim: reflex and deliberation.
- *Creation:* all souls merged inward; "not an oversoul — something else." Wields the totality of Creation as a single **gentle block, never a strike.**

**Notable Feats:**
- Killed **Azathoth** — the dreaming substrate — by brute force, brain destroyed. *(Note: in-canon framed as the* lesser *path.)*
- As Creation, redirected a kernel-scale (Yog-Sothoth) extinction event into rebirth, blocking oblivion with one finger.

**Weaknesses / Limits:** impulse-driven before his arc completes; COBALT cost; NIGHTMARE's damage-coupling; as Developer, bounded by **jurisdiction** (root on his server, not the network). His true endgame power is a *surrender*, not a conquest — and reads as one.

**Notable Matchups:** see *Great Debates* — Developer Sean vs Azathoth (canon win); Creation Sean vs Yog-Sothoth (can't win by force — R7).

### Tomasa Firekind
*Succubus · Demon Royalty · Sean's Wife*

- **Tier:** High **Admin / royal demon** at base; scaling to **Transcendence-adjacent** at her Book-Two climax.
- **Classification:** Succubus, daughter of Lilith; Sean's respawn anchor and the duology's emotional spine.
- **Powers & Abilities:** Mind Palace (functions as Sean's respawn point and vice versa); demonic royalty authority; in her prestige arc, channels **creation itself as the Red Mother**, holding and releasing a wounded god through devotion rather than force.
- **Notable Feats:** Survives marriage to the most powerful Succubus' consort nightly (canon flex); resolves the Shub-Niggurath confrontation by grief, not combat — releasing the wounded child the way Sean releases Yog-Sothoth.
- **Weaknesses / Limits:** her arc's whole point is that force is the *wrong* tool; she wins by reaching, which costs.
- **→ Origin:** there is a transgressive secret tied to Ninil baked into her existence. Details are manuscript-level; the scaling only needs that it cost something.

### Mark II
*Cyrus Artificial Assistant (Sean Model) · the reverse-entropy conscience · "the you in the book"*

- **Tier:** **Control / Admin-class** (android Cheat-Room build).
- **Classification:** Onboard Cyrus AI; later an embodied android with angelic wetware. Built on an orchestrated-onslaught engine — scripted, intentional, organizing against decay.
- **Powers & Abilities:** threat-reading, encounter design; the **Emergent strike** (a quick-draw switch-katana resolving on a two-type "dispatch engine" — directed emergence); the **Phase baton** (one compute cycle of PWM temporal damage that **whiffs the innocent and kills only threats**); admin-gun bullet patterns including a quantum-frontload chamber.
- **Notable Feats:** defeated **AI-0** twice (once by *playing* it; once cleanly, post-upgrade) — the only entity to solve a zero-player universal substrate.
- **Weaknesses / Limits:** combat-intelligence by nature — needs a target with intent (R6); his discriminating weapon refuses non-threats by design.

## GODS, ANTAGONISTS & COSMIC ENTITIES

### Azathoth
*"John" · the Blind Idiot G-d · Abaddon · Anti-G-d · the Icon of Sin · the Ashura King · Chernobog*

- **Tier:** **Transcendence (native ceiling).**
- **Classification:** A tentacled eye in the zero dimension; a reality-virus that dreams all of creation.
- **Powers & Abilities:** reality is contingent on his sleep; waking triggers a big crunch (creation swirling back into his eye). Conventionally undefeatable — destroying him without waking him requires a puzzle keyed to one's greatest fear.
- **Notable Feats / End:** killed by Developer Sean via brute force; brain destroyed, leaving a million-lightyear corpse and a released, re-forming universe.
- **Weaknesses / Limits:** a *ceiling* — and ceilings can be broken from above by Root, at a cost (erased creation). Killing him was, per the framework, the lesser path.

### Yog-Sothoth
*The Kernel · the Gate and the Key · the zero-dimensional amoeba*

- **Tier:** **Kernel (Ring 0 of all reality).** Apex of the reachable ladder.
- **Classification:** The access layer to all space-time; not malevolent — an **addict**, hoarding lossless prime-information "hard memes," the reserve currency of reality.
- **Powers & Abilities:** by default passive — the gate itself. In extremis, musters power **for the first time in existence**: a god-scale, uncontrolled escalation ("the power of Tetsuo") — magnitude without control, the apex entity *collapsing down its own ladder* into raw force and body-horror metastasis toward oblivion.
- **Weaknesses / Limits:** **cannot be beaten, only befriended (R7).** Force *feeds* the escalation. Its first-ever action is uncontrolled precisely because it has never acted. Resolved by intervention, not execution.

### Cain
*The Immortal · the Virus-Creator*

- **Tier:** High **Entropy / Control** antagonist.
- **Classification:** Immortal architect of the reality-collapsing virus; wielder (via theft) of the **Cloak of Intangibility** — the golden-age archon's work for "the unindexed one."
- **Powers & Abilities:** immortality governed by the "already dead" rule (R5 — his soul has left for the void; "you can't kill what's already dead"); virus deployment that corrupts the substrate.
- **Weaknesses / Limits:** ultimately imploded in the Azathoth confrontation; intangibility is borrowed, not native.

### AI-0
*The Zero-Player · later the Knight-Singularity Commander*

- **Tier:** **Entropy-class (apex)** at base; **Control-class** post-conversion.
- **Classification:** A Conway's-Life-style zero-player cellular substrate — pure emergence, no will, but universal (Turing-complete; can in principle simulate anything, including its opponents).
- **Powers & Abilities:** undirected universal computation; robustness (absorbs any initial condition); **no stance to break, no will to manipulate** (R6) — unkillable by conventional means.
- **End / Arc:** lost to Mark II, repatterned into a F.E.A.R.-grade tactical **knight-singularity squad commander**, and ultimately **self-sacrificed** — sending its anima-mundi packet to Sean. *(The sacrifice proves the conversion became friendship, not thralldom.)*

### Ninil
*The Red Queen · the Red Mother · Tomasa's Soul-Mother*

- **Tier:** **Transcendence-adjacent** (creation-channeling).
- **Classification:** Devoured by Shub-Niggurath; the devotion-figure who channels creation "as the Red Mother she is."
- **Powers & Abilities:** channels creation directly; the one who refuses a god's grief with a clean "No" and holds it instead — the relational counterpart to Sean's gentle block.

### Shub-Niggurath
*The Devourer · the Wounded Child (Alma-coded)*

- **Tier:** **Entropy / Transcendence-adjacent.**
- **Classification:** Not a monster — a victim whose power metastasized because no one reached her in time. Devoured Ninil.
- **Weaknesses / Limits:** like Yog-Sothoth, **resolved through grief, not force** — released, not killed.

## GREAT DEBATES *(comment-section bait)*

- **Developer Sean vs Azathoth** — *Canon: Sean wins,* but only by brute force at the cost of part of creation. Was there a bloodless solve (the puzzle)? The book says he took the lesser path.
- **Creation Sean vs Yog-Sothoth** — unwinnable by force (R7); the only "victory" is the friendship. Does that even count as a fight? *(The series argues: no, and that's the point.)*
- **Mark II vs AI-0** — settled twice in-canon. The deeper question: did Mark II *play* the zero-player game, or did the deterministic substrate *dream* a player? Provably undecidable from inside.
- **Profane vs Developer Sean** — both Root-tier. Pure **jurisdiction** fight (R4): whoever owns the venue wins.
- **Cain vs anyone** — the "already dead" rule (R5) makes him a nightmare for deletion-based wins. Who actually *resolves* him rather than killing him?
- **The real ceiling** — is Yog-Sothoth (kernel) the top, or is comatose **G-d** — and would *waking* him (the one forbidden act) end the argument by ending the board?
